The format

Thirty minutes. Three principles. One question.

A repeatable spine so listeners feel the rhythm by minute 8, and a contract that every episode delivers real moments — not theory.

30 minutes per episode
3–4 principles per episode
1 spine question

"What did you have to learn the hard way?"

The Principle Pass — the show's atomic unit. Every principle, every episode, follows these five beats:

0–1 min Read it. One of us reads the principle aloud, in full.
1–4 min The moment. Guest brings the moment in their career when this principle stopped being abstract.
4–6 min The other beat. Co-host brings their version of the same principle — same idea, different lens.
6–7 min The friction. Where do we see it differently? What would you edit?
~7 min The reframe. One sentence: how would you word the principle differently after this conversation?

The Operator and the Coach.

Three principles. Two you already named publicly. One that explains why this show exists. Swap any of them if a different one matters more to you — the doc moves with you.

Principle IV

I Hire the Person Before the Resume

You named this one in your comment as deeply important across your entire career. The pairing is sharp: I hire as an operator inside a $250M ARR platform, you assess character as a coach. Same conviction, different muscle.

Story prompt for you Tell me about a moment where character was the variable that made the difference — for better or worse — on a team you were close to. The hire that should have looked rough on paper. Or the one whose pedigree didn't catch what was missing underneath.

Principle XI

The Fall Is Mine. The Win Is Yours.

The principle you led with. The natural friction in this one is delicious: as a coach, when is taking the fall actually rescuing someone from the lesson they needed? I'll bring the operator instinct to absorb impact. You bring the coach's read on when that instinct stops serving.

Story prompt for you A moment when you watched a leader take the fall — and it either built or broke something. Or, when you've coached someone through the choice of whether to absorb impact or let the lesson land. Where was the line?

Principle XIV

The Unofficial Leader Goes First

The meta-principle. The reason the show exists. You went first — publicly, generously — on a manifesto you didn't have to engage with. This is the principle we're living by recording the episode. It also gives us our natural close: this show exists because someone went first. Subscribe and we'll keep going.

Story prompt for you A moment you went first when nothing required you to. The conversation no one else wanted to start. The vulnerability you led with that made it safe for someone else to follow. What did it cost you, and what did it open up?

Logistics

How we actually do this.

  • Pre-recorded, not live. We record ~45 min for a polished 30-min cut. Lower stakes, higher quality, more clips.
  • Video + audio via Riverside or Zencastr (I'll pick and send the link). Same studio quality you'd hear on any pro show.
  • One prep call, ~30 min, the week before. We land which stories each of us is bringing and how we want the conversation to open.
  • One shared doc — this one, evolving. You edit anything, anytime.
  • Hosted on Substack, distributed to Apple/Spotify/Overcast via Substack's podcast feature. Goes out to my existing Unofficial Leader subscribers as both an email and a podcast drop.
  • You're billed as co-host on the show and every episode. Your name on the cover. Your bio on the show page. Your link on every share.
